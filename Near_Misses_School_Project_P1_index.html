What is a "Near Miss?

Near misses are encounters cyclists have on the road that represented a severe risk of boildy harm. This can be cars pulling out in front of you, a car passing on the left and making a sharp right turn in front you, a car blowing past you at high speed and passing very closely, a person opening a car door into a bike lane, a cars driving extremely close behind you, and similar events. In such events, if things were slightly different, could have resulted in serious injury or death for a cyclist.

Why should we catalog them?

There are few if any tools for collecting data on near misses. There is Bike Lane Uprising which currently allows users to submit a record and photo of an obstructino in a bike lane, a persistant problem. There is How's My Driving Twitter Bot which users can use to find if a driver in the DMV has any citations or unpaid tickets. Hopefully this will motivate DC Police to crack down on habitually unsafe drivers, the ones at a might higher risk of causing an accident. DC Police track accidents involving bicyclists here. This data can also be found on my maps page here. Near misses occur on a daily basis to cyclists all over DC. I personally lost count years ago of my close calls I have had biking in DC. Information on the daily state of living as a cyclist in DC will hopefully motivate politicans and law enforcement to take stronger action to protect cyclists while advocates can add another tool to their arguments in the public sphere.

Other Organizations Efforts

WABA Crash Tracker

The Washington Area Bicycle Association has a crash form you can submit if you are involved in or witness a bicycle here This data is then shared with law enforcement. Unfortunately there are no associated visuals and the database does not appear to be public.

What you can find here

I have created a simple survey using ESRI's Survey123 program that I encourage you to take if you are a cyclist in the metro DC area and have experienced a near miss. The survey is short and should take a few minutes at most to finish. You can add a near miss that happend today or a year ago, anywhere in the area. Your data point will be automatically added to the maps on this website - reload the page and you should see your near miss added to the map. You can also find some links to ArcGIS Online, where these feature classes are stored and updated. Any data collected is intended to be public and available as such.
